What is Implicit Bias?

Implicit bias refers to the attitudes or stereotypes that affect our understanding, actions, and decisions in an unconscious manner. These biases can influence our decisions and behavior without our awareness. Understanding implicit bias is crucial for promoting diversity and inclusion.

Many studies have shown that implicit bias can have an impact in multiple fields, including education, career advancement, and healthcare, leading to unfair outcomes. By learning and reflecting on these biases, we can become aware of our own prejudices and work to eliminate them.

Intersectionality and Implicit Bias

Intersectionality refers to how different aspects of a person's identity (such as gender, race, class, etc.) interact to influence the privileges or oppressions one experiences. Understanding intersectionality helps us to recognize implicit bias more comprehensively, as biases often intersect in various social contexts, leading to multifaceted impacts.
